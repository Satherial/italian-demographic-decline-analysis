# 1.1 Measuring National Identity and Trust in Institutions in Italy

## Part A: National Identity Analysis

### Eurobarometer 508 Study (Oct-Nov 2020)

#### Purpose and Methodology

**Objective**

The report studies:

* **Personal values**
* **Identity dimensions** 
* **Attitudes toward EU values (Article 2 TEU)**

It explores how these interact and vary across countries and socio-demographic groups.

**Methodology**

* Survey conducted **Oct–Nov 2020**
* ~**27,000 respondents** across EU27
* Mixed methods:
  * Face-to-face (CAPI)
  * Online (CAWI) due to COVID-19
* Results are **population-weighted EU averages**

---

#### Conceptual Framework

**Four Value Dimensions (Schwartz-inspired model)**

1. **Self-transcendence**
   * Caring for others, equality, tolerance, nature
2. **Openness to change**
   * Autonomy, independent thinking, new experiences
3. **Conservation**
   * Security, tradition, conformity
4. **Self-enhancement**
   * Power, wealth, dominance

These form two axes:
* Conservation ↔ Openness
* Self-enhancement ↔ Self-transcendence

---

#### Key Findings: Personal Values

**Most Important Values (EU average)**

* Caring for close people (benevolence): **77–78%**
* Making own decisions (self-direction): **78%**
* Forming own opinions: **73%**
* Personal security: **72%**
* Caring for nature: **71%**
* Equal opportunities: **70%**

**Least Important Values**

* Wealth: **13%**
* Power: **22%**
* New experiences: **47%**

**Interpretation**

* Europeans prioritize:
  * **Altruism + autonomy**
* They reject:
  * **Materialism + dominance**

---

#### Aggregate Value Scores (EU level)

* **Self-transcendence: 80% (highest)**
* **Openness to change: 78%**
* **Conservation: 76%**
* **Self-enhancement: 45% (much lower)**

**Insight**

European societies are:
* Strongly **post-materialist**
* Balanced between **security and autonomy**
* Weakly oriented toward **power/wealth**

---

#### Cross-country Patterns

* **Self-transcendence dominates** in most countries
* Exceptions:
  * **Bulgaria, Hungary → conservation highest**
  * **Denmark, Netherlands → openness highest**

**High conservation countries**
* Greece, Bulgaria, Cyprus, Hungary (~84–86%)

**High self-enhancement countries**
* Romania (63%)
* **Italy (57%)**
* Poland (56%)

---

#### Identity of EU Citizens

**Most Important Identity Dimensions**

* Family: **81%**
* Nationality: **73%**
* Gender: **72%**
* Age/life stage: **71%**

**Less Important**

* Being European: **56%**
* Religion: **53%**
* Political orientation: **54%**

**Key Insights**

1. **Identity hierarchy**
   * **Private identities dominate**: Family > Nation > Europe

2. **Weak European identity**
   * Only **56% identify as European**
   * In **8 countries <50%**

3. **Religion is divisive**
   * 53% identify
   * 24% explicitly do not → **highest rejection rate**

---

#### Italy-Specific National Identity Data

**Source 1: Quaderni di Scienza Politica (longitudinal study, 1982–mid-1990s)**

- Between 1982 and 1993, only ~9% of Italians spontaneously expressed a national identity in integrated tests
- This figure is significantly lower than comparable countries in the same period (France, Spain, Argentina)
- Regional identity did not compensate — also weak
- Supranational (European) identity was nearly absent in this period
- Sociodemographic pattern: national identity was more present among men and lower social strata; middle classes were underrepresented
- Around 1994, a notable and structural increase in national identity expressions was recorded
- The author treats this as a durable shift, not a contingent spike

**Source 2: Special Eurobarometer 508 (fieldwork Oct–Nov 2020, N~27,000 EU27)**

- EU average: family 81%, nationality 73%, gender 72% as most important identity dimensions
- Italy-specific disaggregated data on national identity ranking not available in this document
- Italy scores 57% on self-enhancement (power, wealth, dominance) — among the highest in the EU, alongside Romania (63%) and Poland (56%)
- EU average for self-enhancement is 45%
- High conservation countries include Greece, Bulgaria, Cyprus, Hungary (84–86%); Italy is not in this group

## Part B: Trust in Italian Institutions (2024)

### Overall Trend (2024)

In 2024, trust in Italian institutions declined compared to 2023, interrupting a recent upward trend. However, over the longer period (2012–2024), trust in political institutions has generally increased, although it remains relatively low.

---

### Most Trusted Institutions

**Fire Brigade (Vigili del Fuoco)**
* Around 87–90% of citizens express trust (scores 6–10)
* 67.5% express high trust (8–10)

**Law enforcement (Forze dell'ordine)**
* 72.9% trust (down from 76.2% in 2023)
* 40.1% high trust

**President of the Republic**
* 68.2% trust (down from 71.6%)
* 45.2% high trust

These are the only institutions consistently above the "sufficiency" threshold.

---

### Intermediate Levels of Trust

* **Municipal government**: 50.0% (53.2% in Northern Italy)
* **Judicial system**: 44% (down from 46.1% in 2023)
* **Regional government**: 40.9%
* **Italian Parliament**: 40.8%
* **European Parliament**: 40.2%
* **National government**: 37.3%

Trust tends to decrease as the distance between citizens and institutions increases (local > national).

---

### Least Trusted Institution

**Political parties**
* 22.4% express trust
* Over 20% express zero trust
* Around 50% give low scores (1–5)

---

### Long-term Evolution (2012–2024)

* Italian Parliament: from 22.9% to 40.8%
* Regional government: from 25.5% to 40.9%
* National government: from 24.3% to 37.3%
* Political parties: from 9.9% to 22.4%

Despite improvements, trust remains low overall.

---

### Territorial Differences

* Higher trust in local institutions in Northern Italy
  * Municipal trust: 53.2% (North) vs 43.5% (South)
* Regional government trust also about 10 percentage points higher in the North
* Trust in the national government is similar across regions
* The judicial system is the only institution with higher trust in Southern Italy (46.7%)

---

### Demographic Differences

* Trust increases with age
  * President of the Republic: 61.2% (ages 14–34) vs 75.3% (55+)
  * Law enforcement: 66.2% vs 78.1%
* No major gender differences

---

### Education Differences

* Higher-educated individuals show more trust in:
  * President of the Republic
  * European Parliament (about +10 percentage points)
* Lower-educated individuals show more trust in:
  * National government (38.6% vs 35.3% among graduates)
  * Political parties (24.1% vs 20.2%)

---

### Key Takeaway

Trust in Italy is structurally high for non-political institutions (firefighters, police, presidency) and structurally low for political actors, especially parties. Local institutions are trusted more than national ones, and overall confidence shows long-term stagnation despite partial recovery.
