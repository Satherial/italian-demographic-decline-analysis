# 1.2 Comparing National Identity and Fertility Across European Countries Using Open Survey Data

## Methodological Approach

This analysis uses open-access European data sources (European Social Survey, Eurobarometer, ISTAT) to compare national identity and fertility across European countries.

---

## Part A: National Identity and Belonging (ESS Proxies for WVS E001, E003, G006)

### Data Sources and Access

**European Social Survey (ESS) – Data Portal**
- Open access to microdata and online analysis tool for all rounds where Italy participated
- URL: https://esssearch.nsd.uib.no/
- Microdata requires only free registration on ESS portal

**ESS Country Information – Italy**
- Overview of ESS themes and implementation in Italy
- Provides context for Italian participation patterns

**ESS Core Questionnaire on Identity**
- PDF with exact wording and scales for identity items
- Standardized methodology across countries

### Measurable Variables (Parallel to WVS)

The ESS core questionnaire includes:

1. **Attachment strength** to country, region, city/town, Europe
   - Question: "How close do you feel to..." with ordered response scales
   
2. **National and ethnic identity questions**
   - How people see their national vs ethnic belonging

### Constructed Measures

From ESS data we can construct:

- **Strength of national identity** (conceptually close to WVS G006 "how important is national identity to you")
- **Hierarchy of identifications** (local vs national vs European) as proxy for WVS E003 (national pride vs "citizen of the world")

### Verification Method

1. Use ESS Data Portal
2. Select rounds (e.g., Round 6 or 7) and countries: Italy, France, Sweden, Germany, Spain, Poland
3. Add identity variables from core questionnaire
4. Generate percentage distributions or download microdata

## Part B: National Pride Analysis

### Proxy Sources for WVS E001

Since no single open source reproduces WVS E001 exactly, we use:

1. **ESS modules** with pride/evaluation items (checked round-by-round)
2. **Combined measures** from ESS/ISSP datasets:
   - Strong national identity + positive country evaluation
   - Comparable scales and samples across countries

### Quality Assurance

- Check questionnaire PDFs for exact wording, scales, population
- Verify module fielded in all target countries
- Prefer microdata/official tables over secondary summaries

## Part C: Happiness and Subjective Wellbeing (Proxy for WVS A008)

### Key Sources

**ESS Wellbeing Modules**

- Items close to happiness and life satisfaction

**Report: "Il benessere personale e sociale degli europei"**
- ESS report explaining wellbeing measurement

### Conceptual Alignment

- **Single-item proxy**: Close to WVS A008 ("feeling of happiness")

### Cross-verification

1. Use wellbeing report to identify exact items and scales
2. Locate variables on ESS Data Portal
3. Extract distributions for target countries

## Part D: Family, Children, and Fertility Intentions (Proxies for WVS D054)

### 4.1 ISTAT Fertility Intentions in Italy

**Source**: ISTAT "Fertility intentions – Year 2024"

**Key Findings**:
- 21.2% of people aged 18–49 intend to have a child within next three years (down from 25.0% in 2003)
- Among those with one child: ~1/3 of men and >1/4 of women intend to have another child
- Among childless: intentions differ by sex (lower for men, higher for women)
- Among those with 2+ children: intentions for more children are very low

**Advantages over WVS D054**:
- Directly measures intended fertility (closer to actual behavior than normative attitudes)
- Official statistical methodology

### 4.2 Eurobarometer Family Ideals

**Source**: "Childbearing Preferences and Family Issues in Europe" working paper

**Key Points**:
- **Nordic countries** (including Sweden): ideals of 3+ children significantly more common
- **Italy, Spain, Germany**: lower ideal number, 2-child ideals dominate
- **Childless acceptance**: Non-trivial share choose "no children" as ideal even in early 2000s

### 4.3 Italy-Specific Research

**Source**: "Trends in fertility preferences among Italian young adults"

**Core Result**: Rising childfree preferences, especially among women and younger cohorts (2012-2022), against backdrop of already low actual fertility.

### 4.4 Comparative Context

**Source**: "Lowest-Low Fertility in Europe: Exploring the Causes and Consequences"

**Analysis**: Italy and Spain as archetypal "lowest-low" fertility cases, examining structural constraints (labour market, welfare, housing, gender norms) interacting with family preferences.

### Data Integration Framework

**National Identity and Belonging**
- ESS core identity variables → proxies for WVS E003 and G006

**Happiness/Life Satisfaction**  
- ESS happiness and life satisfaction items → proxy for A008

**Family and Reproductive Values**
- ISTAT fertility-intentions indicators (Italy)

- Peer-reviewed studies on trends

### Verification Procedure

1. **Start from original questionnaires/technical reports** for wording, scales, sampling
2. **Use official portals** (ESS Data Portal, ISTAT, Eurobarometer) for actual numbers
3. **Verify secondary summaries** against underlying public datasets
4. **Check academic papers** for clear data source identification

## Part G: Comparative Analysis Framework

### Country Selection
- **Italy**: Primary focus
- **France**: Higher TFR, explicit pro-natality policies  
- **Sweden**: Nordic paradox case, high gender equality
- **Germany**: Similar economic development, moderate TFR
- **Spain**: Southern European, low TFR comparison
- **Poland**: Eastern European, different cultural context

### Key Comparative Variables

1. **National Identity Strength** (ESS)
2. **European vs National Identity Hierarchy** (ESS)
3. **Happiness/Life Satisfaction** (ESS wellbeing)
4. **Fertility Intentions** (ISTAT/Eurobarometer)
5. **Ideal Family Size** (Eurobarometer)
6. **Actual TFR** (Eurostat/ISTAT)

### Expected Patterns (Hypothesis)

- **Stronger national identity** → **Higher fertility intentions**
- **European identity dominance** → **Lower fertility** (individualistic orientation)
- **Higher happiness** → **Higher fertility** (resources for parenting)
- **Nordic countries** → **High gender equality + moderate-high TFR**
