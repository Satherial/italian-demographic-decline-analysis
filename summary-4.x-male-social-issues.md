# Summary: Male Social Issues (4.x)

## NEET Rates (2024)
- Total: 15.2% of 15-29 year-olds | Female 16.6%, Male 13.8%
- Trend 2019-2024: Male declined 20.1%→13.8%; Female 24.1%→16.6%
- EU comparison: Italy 16.1% second highest after Romania 19.3%, 5 points above EU 11.2%
- Age 25-29: Female NEET 26.5% vs Male 16.9% - female driven by family inactivity, male by unemployment
- Regional: South Calabria 26.2%, Sicily 25.7%, Campania 24.9%, Puglia 21.4% vs North 9.8%

## Educational Underachievement
- Women 25-34 tertiary education: 38.5% vs men 25% (13.5 point gap)
- Early school leavers 18-24: Male 12.2% vs female 7.1%
- Basic competency gap: 10.7% male vs 6.7% female lack reading/math/English skills
- Traditional male educational advantage completely reversed

## Social Isolation and Mental Health
- Loneliness 16-34: 11.7% (2018) → 20.5% (2021)
- People living alone feeling lonely: 47.2% (2021) vs 41.9% (2018)
- International: Loneliness highest among young males; 25% males vs 18% women experienced loneliness previous day
- OECD 2025: In-person interactions declined post-pandemic, especially among young men
- Male suicide ~78% of suicides (requires verification); WHO male rate 12.3 vs female 5.6 per 100,000
- Men less likely to seek mental health support; traditional masculinity norms discourage emotional expression

## Digital Dependencies
- 18-24 age: 78.1% habitual social media use; 68.5% feel need to connect frequently; 50.9% feel less alone via digital use
- 43% feel estrangement from real world; 49.7% perceive time loss from social media
- Smartphone dependency: 32%+ adolescents 14-19; problematic digital behavior predominantly male (53.8% in 15-19)
- WHO recognized gaming disorder (ICD-11 2019); predominantly affects males

## Masculinity Crisis
- Traditional provider role challenged by female educational/employment advances
- 54.1% of men state better if man focuses on economic needs, woman on home care
- 53.7% state not capable of domestic work as well as women
- 31.9% state not right to split tasks equally even when both work full-time
- Many young men perceive world as hostile; vulnerability to radical online "manosphere" influences
- Emotional isolation: Masculinity norms associate strength with emotional suppression

## Fertility Implications
- Male social/economic instability delays marriage and family formation
- Educational mismatch: Women increasingly partner with less-educated men or remain single
- Traditional male provider role requirements delay family timing
- Gender conflict between male traditional expectations and female modern opportunities

## Key Insights
- Gender role reversal: Women outperform men at all educational levels
- Social integration breakdown: Men detached from traditional institutions; online activities replace real-world connections
- Mental health crisis: Stigma barrier prevents help-seeking; male-appropriate services needed
- Policy relevance: Need male-targeted educational/employment interventions and mental health services
