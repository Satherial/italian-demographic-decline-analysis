**Measuring trust in institutions in Italy over time (Eurobarometer, ISTAT series on trust in institutions)**

## Overall trend (2024)

In 2024, trust in Italian institutions declined compared to 2023, interrupting a recent upward trend. However, over the longer period (2012–2024), trust in political institutions has generally increased, although it remains relatively low.

---

## Most trusted institutions

* **Fire Brigade (Vigili del Fuoco)**
  Around 87–90% of citizens express trust (scores 6–10)
  67.5% express high trust (8–10)

* **Law enforcement (Forze dell’ordine)**
  72.9% trust (down from 76.2% in 2023)
  40.1% high trust

* **President of the Republic**
  68.2% trust (down from 71.6%)
  45.2% high trust

These are the only institutions consistently above the “sufficiency” threshold.

---

## Intermediate levels of trust

* **Municipal government**: 50.0% (53.2% in Northern Italy)
* **Judicial system**: 44% (down from 46.1% in 2023)
* **Regional government**: 40.9%
* **Italian Parliament**: 40.8%
* **European Parliament**: 40.2%
* **National government**: 37.3%

Trust tends to decrease as the distance between citizens and institutions increases (local > national).

---

## Least trusted institution

* **Political parties**
  22.4% express trust
  Over 20% express zero trust
  Around 50% give low scores (1–5)

---

## Long-term evolution (2012–2024)

* Italian Parliament: from 22.9% to 40.8%
* Regional government: from 25.5% to 40.9%
* National government: from 24.3% to 37.3%
* Political parties: from 9.9% to 22.4%

Despite improvements, trust remains low overall.

---

## Territorial differences

* Higher trust in local institutions in Northern Italy

  * Municipal trust: 53.2% (North) vs 43.5% (South)
* Regional government trust also about 10 percentage points higher in the North
* Trust in the national government is similar across regions
* The judicial system is the only institution with higher trust in Southern Italy (46.7%)

---

## Demographic differences

* Trust increases with age

  * President of the Republic: 61.2% (ages 14–34) vs 75.3% (55+)
  * Law enforcement: 66.2% vs 78.1%
* No major gender differences

---

## Education differences

* Higher-educated individuals show more trust in:

  * President of the Republic
  * European Parliament (about +10 percentage points)
* Lower-educated individuals show more trust in:

  * National government (38.6% vs 35.3% among graduates)
  * Political parties (24.1% vs 20.2%)

---

## Key takeaway

Trust in Italy is structurally high for non-political institutions (firefighters, police, presidency) and structurally low for political actors, especially parties. Local institutions are trusted more than national ones, and overall confidence shows long-term stagnation despite partial recovery.
