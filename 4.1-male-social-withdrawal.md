**NEET data — gender breakdown (ISTAT 2024, verified)**

Sources: ISTAT "Noi Italia" portal: `https://noi-italia.istat.it` — ISTAT audizione al Senato (Il Sole 24 Ore, 2024) — Fondazione Gi Group elaboration on ISTAT data: `https://fondazione.gigroup.it/dedalo/numeri-che-parlano/i-neet-in-italia/`

- In 2024, 15.2% of 15–29 year-olds in Italy are NEET — neither in employment nor in education or training
- Female NEET rate: 16.6%; male NEET rate: 13.8%
- Between 2019 and 2024: male NEET rate declined from 20.1% to 13.8%; female rate from 24.1% to 16.6%
- Italy's NEET rate in 2023 (16.1%) was the second highest in the EU after Romania (19.3%), approximately 5 percentage points above the EU average (11.2%)
- Age 25–29: female NEET rate 26.5% vs male 16.9% — driven primarily by women's greater rates of inactivity due to family responsibilities
- Territorial breakdown: Calabria 26.2%, Sicily 25.7%, Campania 24.9%, Puglia 21.4% — against a Northern Italy average of 9.8% and a Centre-North average of 10.7%

**Interpretive note on NEET and gender:**
Female NEET rates are structurally higher than male rates across all age groups and territories in Italy. The female NEET rate is driven primarily by family-related inactivity; the male NEET rate is driven primarily by unemployment and labour market discouragement. These are structurally different phenomena within the same aggregate category.

**Male educational underachievement (ISTAT 2024, verified)**

Sources: ISTAT "Noi Italia" portal — ISTAT BES 2024 Chapter 2: `https://www.istat.it/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/2.pdf`

- Women aged 25–34 with tertiary education: 38.5% of the total vs 25% of men — a gender gap of 13.5 percentage points consistently in favour of women
- Early school leavers (18–24 year-olds with at most lower secondary education, not in any training): 12.2% of males vs 7.1% of females in 2024
- At the end of upper secondary school, 10.7% of male students lack basic competencies in reading, mathematics, and English, against 6.7% of female students

**Cross-national finding on male loneliness (verified):**
- Barreto et al. (University of Exeter, N=46,054 across 237 countries, *Personality and Individual Differences*, 2020): loneliness is highest among young people, especially males, and residents of highly individualistic cultures — contradicting the common assumption that loneliness primarily affects the elderly
- Gallup data: 25% of males experienced loneliness on the previous day vs 18% of women — gap confirmed across France, Canada, and Turkey

**Digital dependencies — Italy**

**Eurispes Rapporto Italia 2025 (verified — correctly attributed to Rapporto Italia 2025, not to the separate August 2025 digital report):**
Source: `https://eurispes.eu/ricerca-rapporto/rapporto-italia-2025/`

Among 18–24 year-olds:
- 78.1% use social media habitually
- 68.5% declare feeling the need to connect frequently
- 53.3% feel more free to express themselves through social networks
- 50.9% feel less alone thanks to digital use
- 49.7% perceive a loss of time linked to social media use
- 43.0% report a sense of estrangement from the real world

Additional finding (verified):
- Among people living alone: 44.2% frequently feel the need to connect; 36.5% feel less alone thanks to social media

**WHO recognition of gaming disorder (verified):**
- The WHO officially recognized "gaming disorder" as a behavioural disorder in ICD-11 (2019) — linked to pathological use of video games, characterized by impaired control over gaming, increasing priority given to gaming, and continuation despite negative consequences

**ISS data on smartphone dependency among adolescents (verified directionally):**
- Over 32% of Italian adolescents aged 14–19 show signs of smartphone dependency — source cited as Istituto Superiore di Sanità via Eurispes August 2025 report; requires direct ISS primary source verification before use as a stand-alone figure
