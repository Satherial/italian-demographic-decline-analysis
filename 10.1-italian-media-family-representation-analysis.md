## Cultural Narratives: Absence of Positive Parenthood Portrayals and Extinction Rhetoric

- Analyze the representation of family and children in Italian advertising over the last 30 years through a sociosemiotic approach. A 2022-2023 University of Pavia thesis examining a large corpus of Italian commercials found that while Italian family structures have diversified—with the emergence of single-parent families, extended families, reconstituted families, multiracial families, and same-sex couples—advertising often remains anchored to traditional family models. The research question specifically investigates whether, in this context of demographic and social change, advertising narrates evolutions or remains anchored to old models of family representation. The analysis pays particular attention to stereotypes, the metamorphosis of domestic space, and the changing roles of women (historically objectified through stereotypes) and men (often victims of simplified representations) (Rancati, F., *L'evoluzione della famiglia nella pubblicità: Analisi di stereotipi, cambiamenti dei ruoli e nascita di nuove forme familiari*, thesis, Università di Pavia, academic year 2022-2023) .

- Examine how children and reproduction are strategically weaponized in Italian media discourse by anti-gender and pro-family movements. A critical discourse analysis of two recent Italian events—the 2019 pro-family conference "The Wind of Change: Europe and the Global Pro-Family Movement" in Verona, and the 2020 far-right and ultra-Catholic campaigns against updated abortion law guidelines for RU486—reveals three recurrent ideological concepts: the Child as a symbolic figure; reproductive futurism (the framing of reproduction as a moral imperative for societal survival); and the glorification of heteronormative modes of socialization as "positive sociality." These representations weaponize children to portray the heteronormative family as the "natural" aim, the normal condition of living, and the only acceptable social engagement, while defining non-normative sociality as a threat to the traditional family and the (white) nation (Zanatta, F., & Virgili, E., "Leave the Kids Alone: A Critical Discourse Analysis of the Glorification of Reproductive Futurism in Anti-gender and Pro-family Movements in Italy," *Lambda Nordica*, Vol. 27, No. 3-4, pp. 129-156, 2022. *Lambda Nordica* is a peer-reviewed academic journal published by the Centre for Gender Studies at the University of Gothenburg, Sweden) .

- Trace the emergence and evolution of catastrophic demographic rhetoric in Italian media discourse. The term "inverno demografico" (demographic winter) gained prominence in Italian academic discourse following a 2016 article by demographers Roberto Impicciatore and Rossella Ghigi in *Quaderni di Sociologia*, where they analyzed family formation, fertility, and migration dynamics during the economic crisis using ISTAT data. While the concept existed in international debates prior to this, the Italian contribution helped popularize the term domestically. In subsequent media coverage, however, this concept was taken to extremes, with some outlets escalating the terminology to "glaciazione demografica" (demographic glaciation). This shift represents a transition from academic analysis to exaggerated alarmism about demographic dynamics that demographers had already clearly identified (Impicciatore, R., & Ghigi, R., "L'inverno demografico," *Quaderni di Sociologia*, Vol. 60, No. 72, pp. 7-29, 2016; Neodemos, *Tutto quello che avreste voluto sapere sulla fecondità*, ebook edited by Alessandra Minello, 2025) .

- Analyze the psychological impact of apocalyptic demographic framing on individual reproductive agency. Some demographers and social scientists suggest that media alarmism and oversimplification of fertility decline may contribute to "demographic fatalism"—a sense that individual reproductive choices are meaningless in the face of civilizational collapse. The scholarly response emphasizes the need to shift perspective from alarmism to analysis, from "solutions" for the future to observation of the present. Complex demographic phenomena require rigorous exploration beyond slogans or easy recipes, yet media discourse often reduces them to sensationalist narratives that may disempower individuals rather than encourage reproductive choices (Neodemos, *Tutto quello che avreste voluto sapere sulla fecondità*, 2025; Impicciatore & Ghigi, 2016, cited in Neodemos) .

- Document the misuse of demographic simulations for identity-based political rhetoric. In November 2025, Lega vice-secretary Roberto Vannacci cited a "dystopian" simulation by The European House Ambrosetti—a hypothetical exercise projecting the arithmetic consequences of continued birth rate decline without immigration—as evidence that "the last Italian of origin will be born in 2220." Fact-checking reveals that Vannacci misinterpreted the simulation, which was never intended as a literal forecast about ethnic identity. The simulation did not account for immigration, did not distinguish population by citizenship or origin, and was explicitly labeled "dystopian" by its authors. This episode illustrates how demographic data can be selectively weaponized to advance xenophobic narratives of "ethnic substitution" (Pagella Politica, "Vannacci dà i numeri sull'estinzione degli italiani," November 11, 2025) .

- Examine the divergent narratives surrounding the demographic transition in Italian public discourse. While catastrophic framings dominate political rhetoric—with Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni stating that "children are life and hope, like seeds to plant to grow a forest"—policy analysts note a paradox: Italy's traditional labor market weaknesses (low participation rates among women and youth) could actually be mobilized to counter the effects of demographic decline. This alternative narrative, emphasizing policy opportunities rather than inexorable decline, receives far less media attention than apocalyptic scenarios (Jessoula, M., "Inverno demografico come condanna? Per l'occupazione c'è una via di uscita: aumentare la partecipazione di donne e giovani – L'analisi," *Il Fatto Quotidiano*, December 9, 2025) .

- Investigate young people's perception of the future and its representation in media and research. The Istituto Toniolo's *Rapporto Giovani 2023* reveals that despite widespread narratives of crisis, "desire for the future" persists among Italian youth. A significant majority of respondents hope for a closer relationship between school and work to help them navigate choices. The report's subtitle—"Young people no longer believe (almost)"—captures the tension between media pessimism and lived aspirations (Istituto Giuseppe Toniolo di Studi Superiori, *Rapporto Giovani 2023*, with Ipsos and Fondazione Cariplo, published by Il Mulino, 2023) .

- Assess the gap between media representations of Gen Z and their actual attitudes. A 2025 Ipsos-Toniolo study on 815 Italian adolescents aged 14-19 found that, contrary to stereotypes, Gen Z demonstrates notable levels of hope, agency, and moral sensitivity. On the Hope Theory scale (1-5), young people scored 3.61 on "pathway" (ability to imagine solutions to achieve goals) and 3.52 on "agency" (confidence in their own capabilities). On the Moral Foundations Relevance scale (1-6), they ranked highest on "caring and not causing harm" (4.61), "justice and equity" (4.58), and "purity and personal integrity" (4.51). Girls and younger adolescents (14-16) show the highest sensitivity in these dimensions. Researchers emphasize that "behind fragility there is a desire for redemption and the future" (Ipsos & Osservatorio Giovani Istituto Toniolo, *Indagine "Gen Z e Scuola,"* presented at Parole a Scuola, Università Cattolica di Milano, October 18, 2025) .

- Analyze the sources of generational anxiety that media narratives often overlook. The 2025 Ipsos-Toniolo study reveals that school is frequently perceived as a place of judgment rather than growth. Students report that dismissive comments, misplaced jokes, or humiliating grades become daily wounds that undermine self-esteem. Many respond with apparent indifference or defensive arrogance, masking deep vulnerability. High school students experience greater pressure than technical or vocational students, with perceived expectations at home and in class generating anxiety about not being "enough." Additionally, the *Rapporto Giovani 2024* indicates that 55% of high school students report frequent school-related stress. This suggests that the psychological barriers to family formation may originate earlier in the life course than typically considered (Ipsos & Osservatorio Giovani Istituto Toniolo, *Indagine "Gen Z e Scuola,"* 2025; Istituto Toniolo, *Rapporto Giovani 2024*) .

- Document the availability of media archives for longitudinal family representation analysis. The *Rai Teche* archive, maintained by RAI, preserves decades of Italian television programming, including fiction, advertising, and entertainment content, providing material for systematic analysis of how families and children have been portrayed across generations. The archive represents a critical but underutilized resource for tracing the evolution of cultural narratives about parenthood in Italian media. Similarly, academic research such as the University of Pavia advertising analysis demonstrates methodologies for studying family representation in commercial media over time (Rai Teche; Rancati, 2022-2023) .
