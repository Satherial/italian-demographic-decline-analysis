# Summary: National Identity and Trust (1.x)

## Identity and Values
- EU values hierarchy: Self-transcendence 80%, Openness 78%, Conservation 76%, Self-enhancement 45%
- Italy exception: 57% self-enhancement (power/wealth) vs 45% EU average - among highest in EU
- Identity importance: Family 81%, Nationality 73%, European identity only 56%
- Historical weakness: 1982-1993 only ~9% spontaneously expressed national identity vs higher rates in comparable countries

## Institutional Trust (2024)
- Highest: Fire Brigade 87-90%, Law enforcement 72.9%, President 68.2%
- Lowest: Political parties 22.4%, National government 37.3%, Parliament 40.8%
- Geographic gap: Municipal trust North 53.2% vs South 43.5%
- Structural pattern: Trust decreases with institutional distance from citizens

## Social and Gender Dynamics
- Following Second Demographic Transition: cohabitation, out-of-wedlock births, divorce increasing rapidly
- Female employment: 52.2% vs 70.9% men (18.7 point gap) - lowest in EU27
- Women tertiary education: 36% vs 23% men - reversed historical pattern
- Domestic burden effect: Women with heavier chores show lower fertility intentions

## Historical Narrative Impact
- Galli della Loggia: 1943-1945 events produced radical crisis of national identity - nation collapsed when State collapsed
- Gentile: Italians never had common national idea; nation "disappeared" mid-20th century
- Fascist "Battaglia Demografica" (1925-1938) failed: birth rates declined despite pronatalist policies
- Post-1945: Pronatalism equated with Fascism, creating lasting political taboo
- Italy developed no equivalent to France/Sweden long-term family policies

## Fertility Intentions Context
- ISTAT 2024: 21.2% of 18-49 intend child within 3 years (down from 25% in 2003)
- Over 10.5 million do not want children or additional children
- 41.7% desire 2 children (down from 46% in 2016)
- Fewer than half who expressed positive intention in 2016 realized it within 3 years

## Key Structural Insights
- Weak national identity correlates with demographic decline
- Private identities (family) dominate collective identities
- High self-enhancement values may compete with family formation
- Post-war rejection of fascist pronatalism created lasting aversion to demographic policies
