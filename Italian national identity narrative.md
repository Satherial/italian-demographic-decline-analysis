# Italian National Identity Narrative — Corrected Data Summary (Verified)

## Galli della Loggia, La morte della patria (Laterza, 1996)
**ISBN 9788842048671**

The book argues that the events between 8 September 1943 and 25 April 1945 produced a radical crisis of the idea of nation in Italy

* **Central thesis**: the Resistance failed to establish a new shared democratic national identity
* The crisis of national identity is described as "perhaps the heaviest legacy that the lost war transmitted to the Republic"
* The nation in Italy, unlike other states, did not precede the State — it was a product of it; when the State collapsed morally in 1943, the nation collapsed with it
* The book generated a major historiographical debate; critics include Nicola Tranfaglia; sympathetic readings include Renzo De Felice's parallel thesis

## Galli della Loggia, L'identità italiana (Il Mulino, 1998; paperback edition 2010)
**ISBN 9788815139740**

### Main Points

* **Central argument**: Italian identity, product of a millennial history, is still perceived as fragile and has not managed to translate into a unitary idea of the country in modern form
* **Themes explicitly covered**:
	+ Landscape and environment
	+ Latin heritage
	+ Catholic heritage
	+ Urban and regional polycentrism
	+ The individual caught between family and oligarchy
	+ Pervasiveness of politics
	+ Weakness of the state
* **Explicit thesis**: "much politics and little state, much ideology and little culture of the state" (p. 143, confirmed by academic review)
* The Counter-Reformation is treated as a formative event producing a specific type of Italian Catholicism — theme confirmed present, but exact wording of the characterization not independently verified
* The book explicitly distances itself from Banfield's "amoral familism" and Putnam's theses while arriving at structurally convergent conclusions
* The book is the founding volume of the Il Mulino "L'identità italiana" series edited by Galli della Loggia himself

## Gentile, La Grande Italia (Laterza, 2006)
**ISBN 9788842078562** — also available free on Internet Archive: https://archive.org/details/lagrandeitaliail0000gent

### Main Points

* **Central thesis**, confirmed directly from publisher description: "Italians have in reality never had a common idea of nation, even though since the Risorgimento the myth of a Grande Italia has influenced their existence"
* **Covers**:
	+ Risorgimento
	+ Liberal State
	+ World War I
	+ Fascism
	+ Resistance
	+ Republic
* Documents multiple competing and antagonistic versions of the national myth across political families: Liberals, Catholics, Monarchists, Republicans, Fascists, Socialists
* Argues the nation "disappeared from the life of Italians" from the mid-twentieth century onward
* **NOTE**: the specific argument about the Republican period as "systematic demythologization without replacement" is my inference — not independently verified from the text

## Gentile, Il culto del littorio (Laterza, 1993; IX reprint 2024)
**ISBN 9788842063230**

### Main Points

* Documents Fascism's construction of a "civic religion" — a secular political religion built through myths, rites, and monumental spaces
* The Fascist regime aimed to instill in millions of Italians the faith in a new lay religion institutionalized and practiced among the masses
* The book traces this construction from the French Revolution through the Risorgimento to the Ventennio
* **NOTE**: the argument that this constructed identity collapsed entirely in 1943–45 "leaving no organic national sentiment" is my logical inference — plausible given the book's content but not verified from primary text
