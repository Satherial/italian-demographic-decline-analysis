# Summary: Female Employment and Gender (3.x)

## Employment Rates
- 2024: Female 57.4% vs male 76.8% (19.4 point gap) - lowest in EU27
- 1977-2012: Women employed rose 6.15M→9.46M; share of employment 31.5%→41.3%
- Growth 2008-2024: Italy +6 points vs EU average +8.6 points
- Regional: North/Central met Lisbon 2010 target; South no regions met target
- Trentino-Alto Adige 67.8% (exceeds EU average)

## Gender Pay Gap and Motherhood Penalty
- Italy unadjusted pay gap 4.2% (2020) vs EU 13.0% - distorted by selection effects (lower-skilled women exited labor market)
- Monthly gap 16.7% vs hourly 2.2% - reflects concentration in part-time contracts
- Motherhood penalty (Casarico & Lattanzio 2023): 52 log points = ~40-50% earnings reduction at 15 years post-birth
- Probability of non-employment doubles in 2 years post-maternity; gap persists 15 years
- Child penalties larger in regions with traditional gender norms: North 65% combine work/motherhood vs South 45%
- Eliminating child penalty would increase female employment +6.5 points by 2040

## Nordic Paradox Update
- Nordic TFR 2024: Iceland 1.56, Denmark 1.47, Norway 1.44, Sweden 1.43, Finland 1.25
- Nordic paradox (high equality + high TFR) dissolved since 2010: all Nordic countries experienced sharp fertility declines (-22% to -33%)
- Postponement of first births primary driver; rising childlessness; slower parity progression
- McDonald (2000): Gender equity in individual-oriented institutions but not family-oriented institutions produces very low fertility
- Esping-Andersen: "Incomplete revolution" - transition from male-breadwinner to dual-earner equilibrium produces sub-optimal outcomes

## Fertility Intentions (ISTAT 2024)
- 21.2% of 18-49 intend child within 3 years (down from 25% in 2003)
- Age 25-34: 38.5% intend child within 3 years (highest share)
- Age 18-24: 90% do not intend within 3 years; but 81.8% desire children in future
- Fewer than half who expressed positive intention in 2016 realized it within 3 years
- 41.7% desire 2 children (down from 46% in 2016)
- 65.1% of women 15-49 use contraceptive methods

## Domestic Work Distribution
- Women 20-74: 5h 9min daily unpaid domestic work; men: 2h 16min
- Women 25-64: domestic work 21.7% of day (5h 13min); men 7.6% (1h 50min)
- Couple domestic work: 74% done by women (2013-2014), down from 78.4% (2008-2009)
- Childcare daily: fathers 46.8% participate, spend 1h 20min; mothers 73% participate, spend 1h 44min
- Male attitudes: 54.1% believe better if man focuses on economic needs, woman on home care
- Couples with less equitable domestic work division have lower probability of becoming parents

## Employment-Fertility Relationship
- 1970s-1990s: Employment and TFR moved opposite (negative correlation)
- 2000-2008: Both rose simultaneously ("Nordic paradox" pattern)
- Post-2012: Both declined together
- Regional: Employment positively correlates with motherhood in North, weakly negative in South
- Women still in education show lowest motherhood probability
- Stable male employment remains precondition for having children

## Childcare and Policy
- Monthly childcare cost: €300-400; average family allowance: €160 - net cost exceeds allowance by €140-240/month
- Childcare enrollment 0-2 years: Italy ~26% vs EU average 33%
- Regional ECEC coverage variation stronger predictor of fertility than national measures
- Policy priorities stated by respondents: Economic support 28.5%, Childcare services 26.1%, Housing subsidies 23.1%

## Key Insights
- Female employment impact on fertility depends on institutional context, not employment itself
- Gender revolution incomplete: acceptance of working women increased but traditional caregiver role persists
- Italy failed to develop family-support institutions like Nordic countries
- Domestic work inequality independent driver of low fertility
- North-South divide reflects different institutional contexts and gender norms
