# Summary: Motherhood Perception (5.x)

## Historical Evolution
- 1988-2008: Support for gender equality in public sphere increased; support for women's dual role (work+family) declined then slight increase by 2008
- Italy developed gap between public sphere egalitarianism and private sphere traditionalism
- 1990-2020: Egalitarian shift driven by period effects (affecting all ages simultaneously), not cohort replacement - potentially reversible
- McDonald framework: High equity in individual-oriented institutions + low equity in family-oriented institutions = very low fertility

## Behavioral vs Attitudinal Change
- Out-of-wedlock births: ~5% (late 1990s) → 38% (late 2020s), near European average 42%
- Civil marriages: <20% → 50% in last two decades; from 2% in early 1970s
- Cohabitation: "Slowly but steadily become part of family life"
- Behavioral change accelerating faster than attitudinal change measured by EVS surveys

## Parenthood as Self-Fulfillment
- 40% of childless 18-34 would feel fully realized without children (Genus 2025)
- Economic/employment insecurity explains stance but cultural factors independently significant
- Paradox: Traditional women intend more children but don't see motherhood as self-essential; non-traditional women see motherhood as choice but less likely to intend children
- 2016 "Fertility Day" campaign framed fertility as national duty - widespread rejection marked shift from duty-based to choice-based perception

## Contraceptive Attitudes
- France 1978: 60% using modern contraception; Italy 1979: only 16%; Italy 1996: 34%
- Italy = "imperfect contraceptive society" - achieves control through traditional methods (coitus interruptus) rather than modern contraception
- Cultural shift toward deliberate individual choice arrived later than France/Northern Europe

## Gender Equality and Fertility
- "To be positively associated with fertility, gender-equitable attitudes must not only be strongly present overall, but also similarly diffused among women and men"
- Where institutions don't promote work-motherhood combination (Mediterranean countries), persistent low fertility more likely
- Attitudinal change must be accompanied by institutional change for fertility effect

## Key Insights
- Public-private divergence: Acceptance of working women high but traditional motherhood (women as primary caregivers) persists
- Choice vs duty: Historical shift from obligation to option; Fertility Day rejection demonstrates this
- Mediterranean pattern: Similar divergence in Spain, Greece, Portugal
- Policy must address both economic and cultural dimensions; choice framework requires different approach than duty framework
