**Italy — Stated reproductive expectations vs actual fertility: corrected data summary**

Primary source: ISTAT, "Intenzioni di fecondità — Anno 2024", published 22 December 2025
Survey: "Famiglie e soggetti sociali 2024", sample approximately 30,000 individuals aged 18+, fieldwork May–September 2024
Direct PDF: `https://www.istat.it/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Intenzioni-di-fecondita-22-dicembre-2025.pdf`
Interactive portal: `https://demo.istat.it/tavole/?t=intfec&l=it`

---

**Short-term fertility intentions (3-year horizon)**

- 21.2% of 18–49 year-olds — approximately 4.5 million people — intend to have a child within three years of the interview (down from 25.0% in 2003)
- 74.2% do not intend to have a child within three years (up from 71.2% in 2003)
- 4.7% did not provide information
- Over 10.5 million people do not want children or additional children either within three years or at any point in the future

**By age group:**
- Ages 18–24: approximately 90% do not intend to have a child within three years; however 81.8% express desire for children in the future — among young men 87.2% (44.7% certain); among young women 75.5% (37.3% certain)
- Ages 25–34: 38.5% intend to have a child within three years — highest share among all age groups
- Ages 35–44: 21.6% intend to have a child within three years
- Ages 45–49: 95.0% do not intend to have children in the future; among women in this group 96.9%

---

**Gap between stated intentions and actual realization**

- Fewer than half of the women who expressed a positive intention to have a child in the 2016 survey realized it in the three subsequent years — even among those who expressed a certain intention, only a minority carried the project through to completion
- Among those who do not intend to have children within three years, 32.6% still state they want children after that time horizon — this figure is more than 3 percentage points lower than the equivalent value in 2003 (36.0%), representing a structural decline in deferred intentions

---

**Desired number of children**

- 41.7% of 18–49 year-olds who intend to have a child within three years or in the future state they desire two children (down from 46.0% in 2016)
- Territorial variation: in Central Italy 39.7% desire two children; in the South only one third indicates two children as the desired number, and 47.1% cannot express a preference; uncertainty is also high in metropolitan centers (42.0%) and the Islands (40.0%)

---

**Intentions by number of children already had (3-year horizon)**

- No children: 23.6% of men and 29.7% of women intend to have a child within three years
- One child already: 32.4% of men and 26.0% of women intend to have another within three years; 92.4% do not intend to have another within three years
- Two or more children: 5.8% of men and 4.2% of women intend to have another within three years; 97.0% do not intend to have another within three years

Note on the 92.4% and 97.0% figures: both refer to the three-year horizon, not to a permanent renunciation of further children.

---

**Stated reasons for not having children or postponing**

- Economic reasons: cited by one third of those who do not intend to have children
- Inadequate working conditions: 9.4%
- Absence of a partner: 8.6%

**Gender asymmetry in perceived impact of parenthood:**
- Half of women think the arrival of a child would worsen their own work opportunities; among 18–24 year-old women this figure exceeds 65%
- 59.0% of men do not anticipate any effect on their own work situation from having a child

---

**Policy priorities stated by respondents**

- Economic support measures: 28.5% indicate this as the top priority for increasing births
- Childcare services: 26.1%
- Housing subsidies: 23.1%

---

**Contraceptive use context**

- 65.1% of women aged 15–49 use contraceptive methods — the fertility intentions data must be read in this context, as unintended births are substantially controlled and the gap between intentions and realizations reflects deliberate choices, not unplanned events

---

**Generational fertility — cohort data (ISTAT Natalità e fecondità 2024)**

- Women born in 1947 — the last cohort to reach generational replacement — had an average of 2.01 children per woman; among them 10% remained childless
- Women born in 1975, who turned 49 in 2024, had an average of 1.44 children per woman; among them childless women represent almost one quarter of the total (23.0%)
